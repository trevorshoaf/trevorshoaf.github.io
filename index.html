About

I am a PhD student in Biomedical Engineering at Purdue University in the lab of Dr. Elsje Pienaar. My research applies computational and mathematical modeling to infectious disease, with a focus on understanding sub-cellular Ebola virus dynamics and evaluating antibiotic efficacy against non-tuberculosis Mycobacterium infections. I use agent-based models and pharmacokinetic/pharmacodynamic frameworks to study how pathogens behave and how treatments can be optimized. Alongside research, I am committed to mentoring undergraduate students and contributing to diversity and inclusion in STEM.
